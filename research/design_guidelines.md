# Design Guidelines: Memorial & Foundation Best Practices (2025)

## Core Design Principles

### 1. Empathetic Aesthetic
- **Visuals:** Warm, reverent, and inviting. Avoid overly dark or somber tones unless intentionally used for contrast (e.g., footer).
- **Color Palette:** Ocean Deep Blue, Gentle Wave Blue, Sunlight Gold, Pure White.
- **Typography:** Playfair Display (Headlines) for dignity; Inter (Body) for readability.

### 2. Mission-First Storytelling
- **Instant Clarity:** Visitors must understand the "Why" within 5 seconds.
- **Legacy:** Celebrate Stephanie's life and spirit (Light) rather than focusing solely on the tragedy.
- **Impact:** visually demonstrate what the foundation *does* (education, events, coins).

### 3. User Experience (UX)
- **Navigation:** Simple, clear phrases. Avoid jargon.
- **Donation Flow:** Frictionless, fewer clicks to donate. clear "Donate" button visible at all times.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. High contrast text, keyboard navigable.

## Structure Recommendations

### Essential Sections (Single Page Hybrid)
1.  **Hero:** Emotional connection, high-quality imagery (water/light), primary CTA.
2.  **Memorial/Story:** "Stephanie's Story" - Brief, touching, focused on her light.
3.  **Mission:** The "What We Do" - Water Safety, Awareness, Community.
4.  **Initiatives:** Cards for Events, Education, Coin Program.
5.  **Resources:** Downloadable safety checklists, tips.
6.  **Team/Founders:** Building trust with faces behind the org.
7.  **Footer:** Legal, Links, Socials, Donate.

### Mobile-First Approach
- Stacked layouts for mobile.
- Touch-friendly buttons (min 44px height).
- Hamburger menu for navigation.

## Inspiration/References

- **Alex Schwarter Foundation:** Example of tragedy-to-action narrative.
- **charity: water:** Gold standard for minimalism and transparency.
