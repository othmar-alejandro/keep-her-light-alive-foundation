In Loving Memory

Stephanie's Light

On November 7, 2024, a preventable boating accident in the Florida Keys took the life of Stephanie Marie Rodriguez, a 24-year-old woman whose light touched everyone she met. She wasn't just a daughter, sister, and friend; she was the person who remembered your birthday, who showed up when you needed her most, who made strangers feel like family.

Stephanie had dreams of becoming the owner of her own gym and healthy food restaurant, driven by her deep desire to care for others. She loved the ocean, sunset boat rides with friends, and the simple joy of being surrounded by the people she cherished. Her laughter was contagious, her kindness boundless, and her presence made every room brighter.

That November evening was supposed to be another beautiful memory - a day on the water with friends, celebrating life. Instead, it became a tragedy that shattered all her family and an entire community. The accident was preventable. The safety measures were absent. And in an instant, the world lost someone irreplaceable.

Her passing ignited a spark in those who loved her most. Her Mother Magaly along with all her Family and close Friends refused to let her memory fade into silence. They founded Keep Her Light Alive - not just as a memorial, but as a mission to ensure no other family experiences this preventable heartbreak. Through boating safety advocacy, education, and community support, we're working to protect lives across Florida and honor the spirit of a young woman who gave so much love to the world.

"Stephanie's light didn't dim when she left us - it multiplied. Every life we save, every family we educate, every act of kindness we inspire carries her spirit forward."

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our foundation and how you can help.

We are a nonprofit organization dedicated to preventing boating accidents through education, advocacy, and community support. Founded in memory of Stephanie Marie Rodriguez, we work to ensure no family experiences the preventable tragedy of losing a loved one on the water.

You can request a coin through our contact form or at any of our events. Each coin is free and comes with a simple mission: perform an act of kindness, then pass it on. The QR code on the back lets you track the coin's journey and see the ripple effect of good deeds.

There are many ways to help: volunteer at our events, sponsor a safety workshop, share our resources with boaters in your community, or make a donation. Every contribution helps us provide free safety education and support families affected by boating accidents.

Yes! All our safety courses, workshops, and educational resources are completely free. We believe that financial barriers should never prevent anyone from learning life-saving water safety skills.

We primarily serve South Florida, including Miami-Dade County, Hialeah, and the Florida Keys. However, our educational resources are available to anyone, and we're working to expand our reach throughout Florida.

If you or your family have been affected by a boating accident, please reach out through our contact form or call us directly. We provide emotional support, connect families with resources, and offer a compassionate community of people who understand your experience.
